## How to Use This Sheet

- 1) Leave the benchmark scores in place if you only want a first-pass market view; overwrite any score you disagree with once you have customer or product evidence.
- 2) Put your own stack scores in column J. If your system is strongest in a narrow layer, raise the weight for that criterion rather than trying to beat every vendor on every row.
- 3) The most valuable comparison is usually not the total score. It is the pattern of where your system is clearly better, roughly equivalent, or non-comparable by design.
